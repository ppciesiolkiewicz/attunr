## Learn Screens

### Vocal Placement (Part 1)
Vocal placement is the practice of directing your voice to resonate in different parts of your body. Lower tones naturally settle in the chest, mid-range tones open in the throat and mouth, and higher tones lift into the sinuses and head.

### Chest and Head Voice (Part 2)
Your voice has different resonances: chest voice (lower, fuller, felt in the chest) and head voice (lighter, higher, felt in the head and face). Low U warms up your chest voice. Hoo hoo introduces head voice — a light, owl-like sound on a high tone.

### Lip Rolls (Part 2)
Lip rolls are a vocal warmup where you let your lips buzz loosely — like a motorboat — while you sing. They relax your jaw, warm your voice, and encourage steady breath without strain.

### Humming and Resonance (Part 4)
Humming is one of the gentlest and most powerful ways to feel your voice. Close your lips and sing mmmm — feel the buzz in your lips, teeth, and face.

## Vowel Progression Ideas

Progression order: U → OO → OH → AH → EH → EE

- **U** (as in "moon") — rounded, closed, low resonance
- **OO** (as in "boot") — similar to U but slightly more open
- **OH** (as in "go") — mid-open, warm
- **AH** (as in "father") — fully open, brightest
- **EH** (as in "bed") — front vowel, transition
- **EE** (as in "see") — front, closed, bright

### Vowel Transitions
- U → AH: closed to open
- OO → EE: back to front
- OH → AH: gradual opening

## Exercise Ideas by Type

### Pitch Detection Exercises
- Low U (chest voice, 5-6s sustain)
- Hoo hoo (head voice, high, accept "above")
- Hum at various pitches (low, mid-low, mid, mid-high)
- U at various pitches
- Vowel sustains (OO, OH, AH, EH, EE)
- Multi-note sequences (3 rising tones, 2s each)

### Tone Follow Exercises
- Lip roll slides (low→high, high→low)
- Lip roll sustain (hold at one pitch)

### Melody Exercises
- Perfect Fifth interval
- Major Second interval
- 5-tone scale (up and back down)

### Breathwork
- Farinelli (incremental count cycles, maxCount 5-10)
